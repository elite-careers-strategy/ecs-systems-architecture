# Intake to Engagement

This document describes the public-safe ECS intake-to-engagement flow.

## Purpose

The intake flow exists to identify the candidate's real bottleneck before any support structure is proposed.

The core operating idea is diagnosis before prescription.

## Step 1 - Public entry

A candidate, parent, sponsor or institution enters through a public surface.

Common entry points:

- Website
- Referral
- Founder profile
- Article or public explainer
- Institutional introduction

The public surface should explain ECS clearly without over-disclosing internal systems.

## Step 2 - Structured capture

The intake layer captures the information needed to understand fit.

Key fields:

- Candidate stage
- Target sector
- Target firms or outcomes
- Geography
- Timeline
- Current application history
- Interview or assessment-centre history
- Parent or sponsor involvement

The point is to find the bottleneck, not collect unnecessary personal information.

## Step 3 - Diagnostic

The diagnostic identifies where the candidate is blocked.

Public diagnostic model:

- Stage 1 - the candidate is not getting interviews.
- Stage 2 - the candidate is getting interviews but not progressing.
- Stage 3 - the candidate is reaching final stages but not converting offers.

Each stage requires a different response.

## Step 4 - Route design

After the diagnostic, ECS maps the route from current position to offer-readiness.

The route should clarify:

- Primary bottleneck
- Required assets
- Required preparation
- Likely sequencing
- Key risks
- Next action

## Step 5 - Engagement decision

The engagement decision should be based on the candidate's gap, timeline and seriousness of fit.

The written materials should explain the route. Commercial terms are handled privately and only in the appropriate setting.

## Design standard

A good intake system should be:

- Fast
- Clear
- Privacy-aware
- Fit-led
- Governed by next actions

## Public boundary

This document is intentionally high-level. It does not include internal pricing, private sales scripts, CRM IDs, internal automation details or private client examples.
